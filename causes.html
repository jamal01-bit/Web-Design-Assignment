Human Activities

The primary cause of the current climate change trend is the human expansion of the "greenhouse effect" - warming that results when the atmosphere traps heat radiating from Earth toward space.

Major Contributors:

  • Fossil Fuels: Burning coal, oil, and gas for energy and transport is the largest source of emissions.
  • Deforestation: Trees absorb CO2, so cutting them down releases it back into the atmosphere and reduces the Earth's capacity to absorb future emissions.
  • Agriculture: Livestock like cows and sheep produce methane, a powerful greenhouse gas.
  • Industry: Various industrial processes release greenhouse gases like nitrous oxide and fluorinated gases.

The Industrial Revolution

Since the beginning of the Industrial Revolution, the concentration of carbon dioxide in the atmosphere has increased by over 45% due to human activities.
