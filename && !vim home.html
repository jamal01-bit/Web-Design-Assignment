The Science

The Greenhouse Effect

The greenhouse effect is a natural process that warms the Earth's surface. When the Sun's energy reaches the Earth's atmosphere, some of it is reflected back to space and the rest is absorbed and re-radiated by greenhouse gases.

Greenhouse gases include water vapor, carbon dioxide, methane, nitrous oxide, and ozone. Without these gases, the average temperature of the Earth would be about -18°C, rather than the current average of 15°C.

Scientific Evidence:

  • Ice core samples show that carbon dioxide levels are higher than they have been in 800,000 years.
  • Global average temperature has increased by about 1.1°C since the late 19th century.
  • The ocean has absorbed much of this increased heat, with the top 100 meters showing warming of more than 0.33°C since 1969.

NASA's Vital Signs

According to NASA, the planet's average surface temperature has risen about 2 degrees Fahrenheit since the late 19th century, a change driven largely by increased carbon dioxide and other human-made emissions into the atmosphere.
