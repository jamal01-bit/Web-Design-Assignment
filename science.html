The Greenhouse Effect

The greenhouse effect is a natural process that warms the Earth's surface. When the Sun's energy reaches the Earth's atmosphere, some of it is reflected back to space and the rest is absorbed and re-radiated by greenhouse gases.

Key greenhouse gases include:

  • Carbon Dioxide (CO2)
  • Methane (CH4)
  • Nitrous Oxide (N2O)
  • Water Vapor (H2O)

How Human Activity Changes the Balance

While the greenhouse effect is natural, humans have increased the concentration of greenhouse gases in the atmosphere by:
1. Burning fossil fuels (coal, oil, gas)
2. Deforestation (trees absorb CO2)
3. Industrial processes and farming.
