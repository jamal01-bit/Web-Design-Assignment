Alternative Design Evaluation (AC 2.3)

Objective: Evaluate two different designs created to meet the project specification and justify the chosen implementation.

Design 1: Form Structure
In this alternative design, the website would have used forms for navigation The homepage would lead to a few main categories (e.g., "The Problem" and "The Solutions"), and users would have to click through the form to reach specific pages like "Wildlife" or "Global Action." 
The user can stretch the navigation bar very simply by just using their moouse however the website doesn't have a very modern look.

Design 2: Flat Structure (The Chosen Implementation)
The implemented design uses a "Flat Structure" where all 10 major content pages are directly accessible from the primary navigation bar on every single page. This makes navigating through the website easier, which is ideal for an educational resource like a Climate Change awareness site. It ensures that the target audience (students and citizens) can find what they need immediately. However, the navigation bar is busier, requiring careful CSS styling to ensure it remains clean and responsive on mobile devices.

Justification:
I chose the Flat Structure (Design 2) for the final implementation because it maximizes the site's accessibility and performance (AC 4.1). In an awareness campaign, speed of information delivery is critical. By utilizing a sticky navigation bar and standard block layout, I was able to overcome the "busy" look of a flat structure, creating a professional, streamlined experience that meets all the requirements for an interactive multi-page website (AC 3.1). This design better serves the target audience by making the full breadth of the climate crisis visible at a single glance.
